# Case 2 Evaluation Plan

Status: prospective plan for the John Brown metaphor-analysis case.

## Evaluation Questions

- Does the governed artifact chain preserve traceability from paper claims to
  source text?
- Do human gates materially shape corpus, codebook, thesis, and publication
  decisions?
- Do adversarial reviews catch overclaiming, attribution errors, or category
  drift?
- Does process logging improve accountability without distorting research?
- What rework, delay, or documentation burden does the method create?
- Which AI recommendations are accepted, revised, rejected, or deferred?

## Added Fields to Track Where Practical

- event timestamp;
- capture mode;
- proposal source;
- accepted, revised, rejected, or deferred status;
- human rationale;
- evidence reviewed;
- artifact changed;
- epistemic effect;
- rework caused;
- time or effort estimate;
- AI/tool cost where available;
- commit SHA;
- evaluation cutoff.

## Limits

This is a second case, not independent universal validation. Results may show
transfer, failure, or modification of the method, but cannot prove general
validity across humanities research.

## Evaluation Cutoffs

Cutoffs should be tied to exact commit SHAs when findings are drafted or
approved.
