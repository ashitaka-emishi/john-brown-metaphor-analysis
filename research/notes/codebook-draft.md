# Codebook Draft

Status: provisional and pending human approval.

## Metaphor

Code metaphor when one conceptual domain structures another, such as slavery as
captivity, action as rescue, law as judgment, or death as seed. Record source
domain, target domain, wording, and whether the mapping is explicit or
inferred.

## Biblical Quotation

Code biblical quotation only when wording is direct or near-direct enough to
identify a likely scriptural source. Record citation, translation uncertainty,
and whether Brown names the biblical text.

## Biblical Allusion

Code allusion when a passage invokes scriptural language, narrative, figure, or
pattern without direct quotation. If the allusion is thesis-bearing and
uncertain, stop for review or mark the claim lower confidence.

## Religious Typology

Code typology when a person, action, event, or suffering is represented through
a recurring biblical pattern. Do not infer typology from pious vocabulary
alone.

## Providential Claim

Code providential claim when language assigns divine direction, judgment,
timing, preservation, punishment, calling, or purpose. Distinguish Brown's
claim from an analyst's description.

## Role Construction

Code role construction when Brown represents himself or others as servant,
father, captain, witness, instrument, prisoner, martyr, deliverer, defender, or
another role. Record whether the role is Brown's wording, a witness report, or
an analyst category.

## Sacrificial Frame

Do not code sacrifice merely because death appears. Sacrifice requires Brown's
language or a clearly identified interpretive warrant involving giving,
offering, consecration, exchange for a greater good, collective benefit,
survivor obligation, or death made morally productive.

## Diachronic Comparison

Assign every passage to a corpus period. Compare changes in vocabulary,
audience, genre, stakes, and source type before treating a change as development
in Brown's self-representation.

## Confidence Scale

High: wording, attribution, locator, and category are clear.

Medium: source is usable but has genre, transmission, or category caveats.

Low: source or classification is uncertain and cannot support a thesis-bearing
claim without review.

## Alternative Readings

Every thesis-bearing row must include a serious alternative reading. Ordinary
nineteenth-century Protestant idiom, legal rhetoric, military practicality,
editorial shaping, and later memory are recurring alternatives.

## Disconfirming Evidence

Mark evidence disconfirming when it weakens, complicates, narrows, or
contradicts a working hypothesis. Do not delete contradictory evidence to make
the argument cleaner.

## Attribution and Variants

Record whether wording is Brown-authored, signed, copied, reported, edited, or
remembered later. Preserve material variants rather than harmonizing them.
