# Question and Scope

Status: provisional. No John Brown source has yet been acquired or verified.

## Primary Question

How did John Brown use metaphor, biblical allusion, and religious typology in
his surviving writings and authenticated statements to understand his
antislavery mission, his use of violence, the failure at Harpers Ferry, and his
approaching death?

## Compact Question

How did Brown's symbolic language construct his understanding of his actions
and death in the struggle against slavery?

## Scope

The project studies Brown's surviving writings and authenticated statements
from early correspondence through his execution on December 2, 1859. It
distinguishes private letters, public statements, court records, contemporary
reports, derivative transcriptions, and later recollections.

## Exclusions

Later martyr memory, songs, biographies, political uses, and sectional
reception are not evidence of Brown's original wording. They may be used only
as reception history or as warnings about interpretive contamination.

## Interpretive Boundary

The project analyzes Brown's representation of action, duty, providence,
violence, failure, suffering, and death. It does not infer private belief,
secret motive, or unconscious intention without explicit evidence and human
approval.

## Required Gate

Stop at corpus approval before acquisition. The scholar must approve the
question, scope, chronological periods, proposed corpus, source hierarchy,
codebook draft, and acquisition plan.
