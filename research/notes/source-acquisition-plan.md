# Source Acquisition Plan

Status: proposed plan only. Acquisition is not authorized until corpus
approval.

## Priorities

1. Locate Tier 1 manuscripts, authenticated facsimiles, signed letters,
   archival transcriptions linked to manuscripts, and court statements with
   strong provenance.
2. Identify Tier 2 contemporary reports, courtroom accounts, newspaper
   transcriptions, interviews, and documented copies.
3. Use Tier 3 later recollections and secondary quotations only for discovery
   or contextual caution unless the scholar approves a stronger use.

## Proposed Periods

- Formation and baseline
- Kansas and armed resistance
- Preparation for Harpers Ferry
- Capture, trial, imprisonment, and execution

Each period requires scholar approval before being treated as complete.

## Acquisition Rules

Use lawful direct downloads, public APIs, repository metadata, or manual
archival inspection notes. Do not bypass paywalls, authentication, CAPTCHAs, or
technical restrictions. Use dry runs, timeouts, conservative delay, and a
descriptive user agent for network tools.

## Stop Conditions

Stop if authorship or attribution is uncertain, a material quotation cannot be
verified, transcriptions conflict materially, access or rights rules prohibit
acquisition, or a required period lacks adequate evidence.

## Risks to Present at Corpus Approval

- Manuscript access or image-use limits
- Conflicting trial-statement witnesses
- Derivative transcriptions without page images
- Later editorial shaping of Brown's prison language
- Copyright restrictions in modern editions
- Preservation bias in surviving correspondence
