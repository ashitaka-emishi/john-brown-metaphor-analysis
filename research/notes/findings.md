# Findings

Date: 2026-07-16

Status: approved as the Goal 30 findings basis on 2026-07-16.

## High Confidence

1. The Gettysburg Address frames the nation as an inherited proposition
   dedicated to equality.

Evidence: `CLM-0001`; `OBS-0001`, `OBS-0002`

Limit: this is strongest as a claim about the Address's rhetoric.

2. The Address frames the Civil War as a test of whether democratic government
   can endure.

Evidence: `CLM-0002`; `OBS-0002`, `OBS-0015`, `OBS-0026`

Limit: Everett and other Lincoln texts also present war as military and
constitutional necessity.

3. The Address transforms soldier death into obligation for the living.

Evidence: `CLM-0003`; `OBS-0003`, `OBS-0004`, `OBS-0005`, `OBS-0006`

Limit: bodily destruction is largely euphemized and must be restored in the
analysis.

4. Lincoln's broader wartime thought includes more than sacrificial rhetoric.

Evidence: `CLM-0006`; `OBS-0015`, `OBS-0016`, `OBS-0017`, `OBS-0018`,
`OBS-0030`, `OBS-0031`

Limit: Gettysburg remains a concentrated and important public formulation.

## Medium Confidence

5. Sacred register is essential to the Address's effect but must be treated as
variant and conventional.

Evidence: `CLM-0004`; `OBS-0004`, `OBS-0007`, `OBS-0008`, `OBS-0009`,
`OBS-0010`, `OBS-0011`

Limit: `under God` is absent in Nicolay and Hay, and Wills's invitation already
uses sacred/consecratory language.

6. Everett and Lincoln share sacrifice language, but Lincoln's compression
creates a different rhetorical effect.

Evidence: `CLM-0005`; `OBS-0012`, `OBS-0013`, `OBS-0005`, `OBS-0006`

Limit: this claim awaits deeper secondary-context review in later goals.

7. `New birth of freedom` is compatible with emancipation and reconstruction.

Evidence: `CLM-0009`; `OBS-0006`, `OBS-0016`, `OBS-0027`, `OBS-0028`,
`OBS-0029`, `OBS-0032`

Limit: Gettysburg alone does not specify emancipation policy, Black citizenship,
or reconstruction mechanics.

8. Contemporary reception was contested.

Evidence: `CLM-0007`; `OBS-0019`, `OBS-0020`

Limit: the reception sample is limited, and `SRC-0011` is derivative pending
the original newspaper image.

## Low Confidence Or Deferred

9. Lincoln's private belief about providence at Gettysburg.

Evidence available: `OBS-0017`, `OBS-0018`, `OBS-0030`

Reason deferred: the comparative texts show providential thinking, but they do
not prove what Lincoln privately believed while composing or delivering
Gettysburg.

10. Exact delivered wording at Gettysburg.

Evidence available: manuscript witnesses `OBS-0007` through `OBS-0010`

Reason deferred: the five witnesses differ, and final delivery wording requires
separate witness/reception analysis beyond the present claim map.

11. Broad national reception of the Address in 1863.

Evidence available: `OBS-0019`, `OBS-0020`

Reason deferred: the acquired reception sample is too small and partly
derivative/noisy.

## Findings For Thesis Gate

Recommended thesis emphasis:

- Gettysburg transforms death into obligation.
- That obligation serves democratic preservation and refounding.
- Sacred language matters but must be variant-aware.
- Lincoln's understanding of this war is plural, not reducible to sacrifice.

Strongest counterargument:

The Address may be better understood as cemetery convention and wartime
mobilization than as a distinctive theory of war.

Best response:

The thesis should not rest on uniqueness or total theory. It should rest on the
speech's observable sequence and on careful comparison with other Lincoln frames.
