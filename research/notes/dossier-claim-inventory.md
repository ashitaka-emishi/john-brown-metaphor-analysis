## Claims Requiring Heightened Caution

- Any claim about Lincoln's private belief.
- Any claim that Lincoln held a general theory of all war.
- Any claim equating death with sacrifice without the operational criteria.
- Any claim about the delivered wording of the Gettysburg Address.
- Any claim that contemporary reception was representative without a documented sample.
- Any claim that secondary scholarship agrees on the address's meaning.

## Disconfirming Evidence to Seek

- Cemetery dedication materials using the same sacred sequence as Lincoln.
- Lincoln texts that frame war only as legal necessity or military instrument.
- Evidence that "new birth of freedom" had a narrow immediate meaning unrelated to death or sacrifice.
- Contemporary responses that ignore sacrifice, consecration, or obligation.
- Everett or other speakers using essentially the same death-to-obligation structure.
- Private Lincoln writings that resist the public meanings proposed at Gettysburg.
- Soldier writings, if later approved, that challenge the purposes attributed to the dead.
