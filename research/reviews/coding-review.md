# Coding Review

Date: 2026-07-16

## Review Standard

This review checks whether the first Goal 20 evidence pass keeps source text,
observation, interpretation, and conclusion separate. It also flags evidence
that weakens or qualifies the working thesis.

## Findings

No row currently presents interpretation as quotation. The `passage` field is
reserved for source language, while analytic claims appear in `observation`,
`interpretation`, and `alternative_reading`.

Every thesis-bearing row includes an alternative reading. The most important
alternatives are:

- founding language may be patriotic compression rather than political ontology;
- `gave their lives` may be conventional commemorative diction;
- consecration language may belong partly to cemetery ceremony convention;
- `new birth of freedom` may be intentionally open-ended;
- Everett's shared sacrifice language limits claims about Lincoln's uniqueness.

Contradictory or limiting evidence remains visible:

- `OBS-0013` shows Everett's stronger military-necessity frame;
- `OBS-0014` restores bodily reality that Lincoln's speech mostly euphemizes;
- `OBS-0017` and `OBS-0018` complicate sacrifice with providence and judgment;
- `OBS-0019` records hostile contemporary reception.

## Risks

The strongest immediate risk is quotation finality. This pass uses local
normalized text, but several normalized files derive from OCR or human
transcription. Final paper quotations from `SRC-0005`, `SRC-0006`, `SRC-0041`,
and `SRC-0042` should be checked against page images. `SRC-0011` should not be
used as final quotation evidence until the original newspaper image is acquired.

The second risk is overextension. Current evidence supports claims about
Lincoln's public Gettysburg rhetoric and the coded comparative Lincoln corpus.
It does not prove a single private or universal Lincoln theory of war.

## Recommendation

Proceed to analysis only with the limiting claims preserved. Do not lock the
thesis until the next stage tests whether additional comparative passages or
secondary scholarship materially alter the evidence balance.
