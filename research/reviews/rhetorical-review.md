# Rhetorical Review

Date: 2026-07-16

Artifact reviewed: `paper/paper.qmd`

## Result

The draft's main rhetorical claim survives review: Gettysburg's force lies in
the arrangement of familiar ceremonial materials rather than in Lincoln's
invention of sacred or sacrificial language. The paper should make that
limitation visible in the conclusion.

## Critique Decisions

### Ceremonial convention is mistaken for theory

Decision: partially accepted.

Evidence: the paper already foregrounds David Wills's invitation, Everett's
main oration, cemetery dedication convention, and the division of labor between
Everett and Lincoln. The counterevidence section explicitly says convention is
not the opposite of meaning.

Problem: the conclusion's final sentence could still sound as if Gettysburg
itself supplies a full Lincoln theory.

Revision required: revise the conclusion so Lincoln's achievement is described
as a public arrangement of familiar ceremonial materials.

### Sacrifice is imposed rather than demonstrated

Decision: rejected with caution.

Evidence: the introduction defines sacrifice restrictively and rejects death
alone as sufficient. The body of the paper then traces giving, national life,
consecration, increased devotion, unfinished work, the vow that the dead not
have died in vain, and new birth. That sequence demonstrates sacrifice as a
rhetorical construction rather than imposing the label on every death.

Caution: the paper must keep this definition in view and avoid using
"sacrifice" as a synonym for death.

### Lincoln is not adequately distinguished from Everett

Decision: partially accepted.

Evidence: the historical-context and counterevidence sections distinguish
Everett's long historical, military, and analogical argument from Lincoln's
compressed sequence of founding, testing, death, obligation, and rebirth.

Problem: the conclusion can do more to preserve that comparison so the final
claim does not overstate Lincoln's uniqueness.

Revision required: add Everett/convention language to the conclusion.

## Remaining Rhetorical Limits

- The argument should not depend on calling the Address unique in vocabulary.
- The stronger claim is about compression, sequence, and public assignment of
  duty to the living.
