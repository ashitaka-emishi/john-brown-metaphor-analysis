# Goal 30 — Analyze the Evidence and Test the Thesis

## Objective

Produce an evidence-led argument map and thesis that survives explicit falsification attempts.


## Operating contract

Read `AGENTS.md`, the `$scholarly-research` skill, and the files named below.
Work in checkpoints. Update `logs/progress.md`.
Do not proceed past a human gate.
Run `python -m lincoln_research validate` and `pytest` before completion.


## Required work

1. Populate `research/data/claims.csv`.
2. Create `research/notes/argument-map.md`.
3. Compare constitutional preservation, democratic test, sacrifice, emancipation, providential uncertainty, and judgment.
4. Create `research/reviews/disconfirmation-review.md`.
5. Steelman at least five competing interpretations.
6. Identify what Gettysburg establishes directly and what requires comparative evidence.
7. Draft `research/notes/provisional-thesis.md`.
8. Produce `research/notes/findings.md` with confidence labels.

## Completion criteria

- Every thesis clause maps to support and counterevidence.
- Thesis distinguishes this war from war in general.
- Analysis does not infer private belief solely from rhetoric.
- Contradictions are surfaced.
- Validation and tests pass.

## Stop condition

Stop at the **thesis approval human gate** and present the thesis, strongest counterargument, unresolved evidence, and proposed revisions.
